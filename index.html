How to write notes by hand
  • Letters are notes: C D E F G A B — uppercase is the lower octave, lowercase c d e the higher.
  • Longer note: add a number — C2 lasts twice as long, C4 is a whole note. Half-length is C/2.
  • Bar line: | tidies the music into measures.
  • Rest (silence): the letter z.
  • Sharp / flat: ^C is C-sharp, _B is B-flat.
  • Up / down an octave: c' climbs, C, drops.
  • Chord (notes together): [CEG] plays a C major chord.
  • Tie / slur: C-C ties; (CDE) slurs a phrase.
  • Pick a Key so you don't type sharps on every note, and a Time signature for waltz (3/4) or jig (6/8). Transpose shifts the whole piece up or down to fit a voice or instrument.
